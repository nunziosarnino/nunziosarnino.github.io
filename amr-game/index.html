CLINICAL GUIDELINES 2026

1. GENERAL PRINCIPLES

‚Ä¢ Start Smart: Use narrow spectrum whenever possible.

‚Ä¢ Biomarkers: Use Procalcitonin (PCT) to distinguish bacterial vs viral.

  • PCT < 0.25 ¬µg/L: Viral likely (STOP Antibiotics).
  • PCT > 0.5 ¬µg/L: Bacterial likely.
2. URINARY TRACT (UTI)

Cystitis (Lower UTI):

  • First Line: Nitrofurantoin 50mg q6h OR Pivmecillinam 400mg TID.
  • CONTRAINDICATION: Do NOT use Nitrofurantoin if eGFR < 45 mL/min (Risk of toxicity/inefficacy). Use Pivmecillinam.

Pyelonephritis (Upper UTI):

  • Requires systemic levels. Use Cefuroxime 750mg-1.5g IV.

Asymptomatic Bacteriuria (ASB):

  • Positive culture but NO symptoms? DO NOT TREAT. (Except in pregnancy/urological procedures).
3. RESPIRATORY TRACT

Community Acquired Pneumonia (CAP):

  • First Line: Penicillin V (Phenoxymethylpenicillin).
  • ALLERGY ALERT: If Penicillin Allergy (Rash/Anaphylaxis), switch to Cefuroxime or Macrolide (Clarithromycin).
4. SEPSIS / UNKNOWN
  • Empiric Broad Spectrum (e.g. Meropenem/Pip-Taz) is allowed initially if septic shock present.
  • De-escalate immediately once microbiology is known.
